About

I am a Ph.D. candidate focusing on the development and use of computational approaches in the neurobiology field. My research focus is gene regulation of nervous system development especially the evolution of neuronal differentiation. To understand nervous system development, I am mainly using multi-omic data analysis techniques such as scRNAseq, scATACseq, and ChIPseq by extensively usage of Linux (HPC clusters and bash scripting), R, and Python. I am also Linux enthusiastic and interested in web development.

Research Projects

The main PhD project is based on transcriptomic comparison of neuronal differentiation between different species such as human, mouse, drosophila, C.elegans and other non-human primates regulated by ETS transcription factor family. In this project, I am mainly constructing ETS mediated gene regulatory network on neuronal diffentiation samples (NSCs/NPCs vs Neurons) and comparing biological features accross different species (Under manuscript preparation).

In this project, transcriptional profiling diffrent stages of glioma were investigated using gene regulatory network inference algorithms for ETS transcription factor family and regulatory patterns of glioma stages were compared in the context of ETS regulation (Babal et al. 2021)

In this project, transcriptomic profiles of gray and white matter tissue samples from multiple sclerosis patients were compared and cell-cell interaction network were constracted using integration of RNAseq data with protein-protein interaction (PPI) networks (Yurduseven and Babal et al. 2022,Accepted paper).
